    <h1>World War I (1914â€“1918)</h1>
    <p>World War I, also known as the <strong>Great War</strong> or the <strong>War to End All Wars</strong>, was a global conflict that lasted from July 28, 1914, to November 11, 1918. It involved most of the world's great powers, assembled into two opposing alliances:</p>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>Allied Powers:</strong> Led by Britain, France, Russia, Italy, and later the United States.</li>
        <li><strong>Central Powers:</strong> Led by Germany, Austria-Hungary, the Ottoman Empire, and Bulgaria.</li>
    </ul>

    <div class="stats-box">
        <strong>Conflict Impact:</strong> 17 million deaths total (9 million military personnel and 8 million civilians).
    </div>

    <h2>Key Causes of World War I</h2>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>Alliances:</strong> A complex web including the Triple Entente and Triple Alliance.</li>
        <li><strong>Imperialism and Nationalism:</strong> Competition for territories and rising national pride.</li>
        <li><strong>Militarism:</strong> An intense arms race and buildup of military forces.</li>
        <li><strong>Assassination:</strong> The trigger event on June 28, 1914, of Archduke Franz Ferdinand by a Bosnian Serb nationalist.</li>
    </ul>

    <h2>Major Events</h2>
    <h3>The Western Front</h3>
    <p>Marked by trench warfare and a bloody stalemate, including:</p>
    <ul>
        <li>Battle of the Marne (1914)</li>
        <li>Battle of Verdun (1916)</li>
        <li>Battle of the Somme (1916)</li>
        <li>Battle of Passchendaele (1917)</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>The Eastern Front & Global Spread</h3>
    <ul>
        <li>Significant fighting between Russia and the Central Powers.</li>
        <li><strong>Russian Revolution (1917):</strong> Led to Russia's withdrawal in 1918.</li>
        <li>Conflict spread to the Middle East and Africa.</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>US Entry and the End of War</h3>
    <p>The US entered in April 1917 following U-boat attacks and the Zimmermann Telegram. The Hundred Days Offensive in 1918 forced the Central Powers into collapse, leading to the Armistice on November 11, 1918.</p>

    <h2>Aftermath and Legacy</h2>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>Treaty of Versailles (1919):</strong> Imposed harsh penalties on Germany.</li>
        <li><strong>League of Nations:</strong> Created to prevent future conflict, though largely ineffective.</li>
        <li><strong>Technological Advancements:</strong> Introduction of tanks, aircraft, submarines, and chemical weapons.</li>
    </ul>
